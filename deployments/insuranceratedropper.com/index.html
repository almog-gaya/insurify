Massachusetts Launches New Policy For Cars Used Less Than 75 Miles/Day

Drivers in Massachusetts are astonished by this new insurance regulation that could save them hundreds

The "Low-Mileage Advantage" Program Is Helping Thousands Save

Recent insurance regulation changes in Massachusetts have led to significant savings for drivers who use their vehicles less than 75 miles per day. This new "Low-Mileage Advantage" program allows qualifying drivers to access dramatically reduced insurance rates that weren't previously available.

Insurance companies have traditionally charged similar rates regardless of how much you actually drive your car. Now, they are required to offer substantial discounts to drivers who can demonstrate less frequent usage of their vehicles.

Many Massachusetts residents have reported savings of 30-50% on their auto insurance premiums after qualifying for this program.

Do You Drive Less Than 75 Miles Per Day?

If you're like most Massachusetts drivers, you probably don't drive your car as much as you think. Recent studies show the average Massachusetts driver uses their car for just 37 miles per day, which means most drivers easily qualify for this new discount program.

Even if you occasionally drive more than 75 miles, you may still qualify based on your average usage over time. The program is designed to be flexible and accommodating to various driving patterns.

The verification process is simple and can be done entirely online without any inspection or tracking devices required in many cases.
